Immigration Law

There are two most complex areas of law in the United States: the tax code and immigration law. However, people tend to associate the practice of immigration law with filling out forms and operating a papermill. This should not give you the false impression that you do not need an immigration lawyer to do just that, because people stumble into the very pitfalls that they don't know exist.

We are located in an immigrant-built city and serve a community who proudly calls themselves immigrants. Let our years of experience with ICE officers, Immigration Judges, and USCIS officers be on your side to each milestone en route to realizing your American Dream.

Criminal Law

“Each of us is more than the worst thing we’ve ever done.”
Bryan Stevenson, Just Mercy: A Story of Justice and Redemption

Everybody deserves a second chance, even the ones who cannot pay their lawyers. This is why our system affords virtually everyone a free lawyer when facing criminal charges. Contrary to popular belief, most public defenders are even more experienced than private practitioners and they can and will push for the best result for you.

What distinguishes our criminal practice is that we strikes a balance between bringing our clients swiftly results (hence less legal fees!) and the practical implications of plea deals: We care about how it impacts your immigration status, work eligibility, and child custody, and endeavors to obtain what is best for you.

Labor and Employment Law

We have all been employed and we know what it's like with bosses. They hold the key to our livelihood and many fail to carry such great responsibility. Capitalism is eclipsing at the bond people used to develop with their masters and their servants.

We are equipped with years of expertise with these age-old conflicts and are here to help you resolve some of the most feared lawsuits by American employers.
